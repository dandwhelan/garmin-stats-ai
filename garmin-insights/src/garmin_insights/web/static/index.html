Lifestyle & Health Insights

Pre-symptom Illness Radar iEarly illness detection (Quer et al. 2021) using z-scores vs your 30-day baseline: elevated RHR, suppressed HRV, and elevated respiration rate. A composite z-score above 2 may be a pre-symptomatic illness signal — before you feel sick.

RHR + HRV-inverted + respiration z-scores (Quer 2021)

Recovery Debt iTracks cumulative energy deficit when your morning Body Battery falls below your personal baseline. Persistent debt means your body isn't recovering fully between days — common during illness, overtraining, or high stress periods.

Inflammation Index iA composite z-score combining elevated RHR, elevated respiration rate, and reduced sleep efficiency. Higher values suggest systemic stress, illness, or heavy recovery demands on your body.

RHR + respiration − sleep efficiency, z-scored

Sleep Regularity Index iMeasures how consistent your sleep timing is day-to-day (0–100). Calculated as the probability that you're asleep at any given minute today vs the same minute yesterday, averaged over 7 days (Windred 2024). High scores are linked to better metabolic and cardiovascular health — even independent of sleep duration.

≥87 regular · <60 irregular

7-day rolling consistency of sleep midpoint (Windred 2024)

Social Jet Lag iYour internal body clock (circadian rhythm) is anchored by consistent sleep timing. "Social jet lag" is the difference in your sleep midpoint (halfway between bedtime and wake time) on weekdays vs weekends. If you sleep at different times on weekends, your body experiences something like weekly jet lag — without going anywhere. A gap >1 hour is linked to metabolic dysfunction, mood issues, and cardiovascular risk.

Weekday vs weekend sleep midpoint

Stress Resilience Score iHow effectively your body recovers from daily stress, measured as your overnight body battery recovery rate relative to your 30-day stress baseline. Higher scores mean your autonomic nervous system is bouncing back efficiently. Chronic low scores suggest you're accumulating stress faster than you're recovering.

Daily, normalised against your 30-day stress baseline

Body Battery Decay Slope iHow fast your body battery depletes during waking hours, expressed as points per hour. A steep drop (large negative number) means your autonomic nervous system is draining quickly — usually from high stress, intense activity, or poor recovery. Flatter decay = more efficient energy management.

Points lost per hour over the day — steeper = faster autonomic drain

Behavior Recovery Cost iFor each logged behavior, this shows the median number of days for your HRV to return within 0.5 standard deviations of your personal baseline. Longer bars = harder impact on your recovery. Helps you understand which behaviors cost the most recovery time.

Median days for HRV to return within 0.5σ of baseline after each behavior

VO2 Max / Fitness Age iGarmin estimates your biological fitness age from VO2 max (your body's maximum oxygen uptake capacity). A fitness age below your real age means above-average aerobic capacity for your demographic. "Achievable fitness age" is Garmin's modelled target ceiling — if your current fitness age is already lower than it, you've surpassed the benchmark. This is a good thing.
