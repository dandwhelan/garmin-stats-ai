Sleep Architecture i[Tier B, medium-confidence measurement] Device-estimated deep, REM, light and awake minutes per night. Consumer wearables differ meaningfully from polysomnography (Chinoy 2021; Schyvens 2024 Garmin validation) — Garmin tends to overestimate light and underestimate deep in some studies. Read as a personal trend vs your own baseline, not clinical staging. Do not treat the 13–23% deep-sleep population norm as a clinical deficit.

Menstrual Cycle i[Tier B] Current cycle phase, day-of-cycle and predicted length from Garmin's Women's Health tracking. Luteal phase typically shows +2–5 bpm RHR and lower rMSSD HRV; late-luteal/PMS days are associated with reduced device-estimated deep/REM and more awakenings. Use cycle phase as a confounder/context label, not a single cause — the same pattern can resemble illness, alcohol, training strain or sleep debt.

Research Signal Scorecard

At-a-glance synthesis of research-aligned markers before chart deep dives

Pre-symptom Illness Radar i[Tier B] Illness-like recovery strain pattern — composite z-score of elevated RHR, suppressed HRV and elevated respiration vs your 30-day baseline. Not diagnostic. The same pattern can follow alcohol, heat, poor sleep, late training, travel, DOMS, emotional stress or luteal-phase physiology.

Sources: Quer 2021 (Nat Med); Radin 2020, Mishra 2022 (Lancet Digital Health systematic review — performance varies, treat as screening, not diagnosis).

RHR + HRV-inverted + respiration z-scores (Quer 2021)

Recovery Debt i[Tier B] Cumulative deficit when your morning Body Battery falls below your personal baseline. Persistent debt means recovery isn't keeping pace — context: illness-like strain, overreaching, alcohol load, or sustained poor sleep. Body Battery is HRV-derived, so the same caveats as Stress apply.

Inflammation Index i[Tier C] Composite physiological strain z-score (RHR + respiration − sleep efficiency). This is not a measurement of inflammatory biomarkers — Garmin can't measure CRP, cytokines or white-cell counts. Use as a deviation-from-baseline trend, not a clinical inflammation reading.

RHR + respiration − sleep efficiency, z-scored

Sleep Regularity Index i[Tier A] Measures how consistent your sleep timing is day-to-day (0–100). Calculated as the probability you're asleep at any given minute today vs the same minute yesterday, averaged over 7 days (Windred 2024, Nature). High scores are linked to better metabolic and cardiovascular health — even independent of sleep duration.

≥87 regular · <60 irregular

7-day rolling consistency of sleep midpoint (Windred 2024)

Social Jet Lag i[Tier B] Difference in your sleep midpoint (halfway between bedtime and wake) on weekdays vs weekends. A gap >1 hour ("social jet lag") is associated with metabolic dysfunction, mood issues and cardiovascular risk, independent of total sleep duration. Wittmann 2006 (Chronobiol Int).

Weekday vs weekend sleep midpoint

Stress Resilience Score i[Tier B] Overnight recovery relative to your 30-day baseline of HRV-derived autonomic strain. Higher = your autonomic nervous system is bouncing back efficiently. Chronic low scores mean strain is outpacing recovery. Note Garmin "stress" is autonomic, not mental — alcohol, heat, illness, late training, and luteal-phase physiology all push it up.

Daily, normalised against your 30-day stress baseline

Body Battery Decay Slope i[Tier B] How fast Body Battery depletes during waking hours, in points/hour. A steep drop (large negative number) means autonomic strain is draining you faster than usual — from high physiological stress, intense activity, illness-like patterns, or poor sleep. Flatter decay = better-paced day.

Points lost per hour over the day — steeper = faster autonomic drain

Behavior Recovery Cost i[Tier B] For each logged behaviour, median days for your HRV to return within 0.5σ of your personal baseline. Longer bars = larger impact on your recovery. Personalised analytic — uses your own baseline, not population norms.

Median days for HRV to return within 0.5σ of baseline after each behavior

Behavior Dose-Response i[Tier B] How quantities of a behaviour correlate with next-night sleep, HRV, deep sleep and RHR. Only meaningful for numeric quantities (e.g. "Alcohol: 2 drinks"). Binary yes/no behaviours pile at x=0. Note: device-estimated deep sleep is a personal trend, not a clinical staging measurement.

Stress Trigger Leaderboard i[Tier B] Behaviours over-represented on your top-quintile autonomic-strain days. Garmin "stress" is HRV-derived strain, not mental stress — read this as "behaviours that coincide with high physiological load". Correlational, not causal.

Step-Count Survival Curve i[Tier A] Distribution of your daily step counts. Read as "X% of my days I walked fewer than Y steps." Paluch 2022 (Lancet Public Health) — ~7,500 steps/day is associated with significant all-cause mortality reduction, with further benefit up to ~10,000; gains plateau after.

Reference lines: 7,500 (mortality benefit) / 10,000 (Paluch 2022)

WHO Activity Target i[Tier A] WHO recommends ≥150 min/week moderate or ≥75 min vigorous (vigorous counts double). Bars stack moderate + vigorous×2 minutes per week; dashed line = 150-min threshold. Bull et al. 2020 (BJSM) — consistent weeks above target are associated with reduced risk of heart disease, diabetes and depression.

Stress Hour-of-Day Fingerprint i[Tier B] Average HRV-derived autonomic strain by hour of day, weekday vs weekend. Garmin "stress" is autonomic, not mental — read the curve as physiological load. Useful for spotting consistent high-strain hours (post-coffee, post-commute, late evening).

Average autonomic strain per hour, weekday vs weekend

VO2 Max / Fitness Age i[Tier A] Garmin estimates biological fitness age from VO2 max (your body's maximum oxygen uptake). Below your real age = above-average aerobic capacity for your demographic. "Achievable fitness age" is Garmin's modelled ceiling — surpassing it is a good sign.

Han 2024 (BJSM overview of meta-analyses, >20M observations) confirms cardiorespiratory fitness is one of the strongest predictors of all-cause mortality.

Vitals by Menstrual Phase i[Tier B] RHR, overnight HRV, sleep score and morning Body Battery averaged within each menstrual phase. Expected pattern: RHR lowest in follicular, rising 2–5 bpm in luteal; HRV typically drops in late luteal — normal physiology, not illness or overtraining.

Use phase as a confounder before attributing changes to a single cause.

Sources: Shilaih 2017 (Sci Rep); Maijala 2022 (Oura); Alzueta 2022; Symons Downs 2025 (Sports Med SR); Nakagawa 2020.

Per-phase averages over the selected window (only phases with data shown)

Cycle-Day Curve (RHR & HRV) i[Tier B] Your sleeping RHR and overnight HRV averaged at each day-of-cycle. Look for the biphasic pattern: follicular trough (days 6–13), ovulatory inflection, luteal rise (days 18–28). ML models can classify cycle phase from sleeping HR alone.

Caveat: reduced sleep raises RHR independent of phase — control for sleep duration before attributing changes to your cycle.

Sources: Shilaih 2017 (Sci Rep); Symons Downs 2025 (Sports Med SR); Masuda 2025 (cycle-phase classification from sleeping HR).

Averaged across recent cycles in window

Sleep Architecture by Phase i[Tier B, medium-confidence measurement] Average minutes of device-estimated deep, REM, light, and awake sleep within each menstrual phase. Late-luteal/PMS commonly shows reduced REM and more awake time; follicular phase tends to have the most restorative sleep.

Consumer-wearable sleep-stage estimates differ meaningfully from polysomnography (Chinoy 2021; Schyvens 2024 Garmin validation) — read this as a personal trend across phases, not clinical staging.

Sources: Baker 2007 (Sleep Med Rev); PMS & sleep quality cross-sectional, 2025 (PMC11842786).

Per-phase sleep stage averages (minutes)

Stress & Body Battery by Phase i[Tier B] Average daily autonomic-strain ("stress") %, high-strain %, lowest Body Battery and Body Battery drained per day within each phase. Autonomic strain often elevates in late-luteal/PMS alongside lower Body Battery recovery. Garmin "stress" is HRV-derived, not validated mental stress.

Sources: Schmalenberger 2020 (Front Endocrinol — HRV across cycle); Symons Downs 2025 (Sports Med SR — wearable HRV across reproductive life stages).

Per-phase averages (only phases with data shown)

Cycle Length — Year Over Year iLength of every cycle in the last 365 days, alongside Garmin's predicted length and the typical 21–35-day reference band (ACOG). Sustained drift outside this range, or a swing >7 days between cycles, is worth discussing with a clinician.

Source: ACOG Committee Opinion 651 (2015) — Menstruation as a Vital Sign.

Last 365 days · always year-scale regardless of dashboard date range

Per-Cycle Vital Averages iMean RHR, overnight HRV, sleep score and morning Body Battery for each cycle in the last 365 days, with a linear trendline. A persistent upward drift in RHR or downward drift in HRV across cycles can flag chronic stress, illness, or training overload independent of cycle-phase noise.

Sources: Plews 2013 (IJSPP); Shilaih 2017 (Sci Rep).

One point per cycle, linear trendline overlaid

Phase Duration per Cycle iDays spent in each phase per cycle. A consistently short luteal phase (<10 days) can indicate low progesterone; a lengthening follicular phase can indicate delayed ovulation. Patterns trend more meaningfully than single cycles.

Source: Kuhl 2005 (Climacteric); Vollman 1977 (Major Probl Obstet Gynecol).
