Lifestyle & Health Insights

Pre-symptom Illness Radar iEarly illness detection (Quer et al. 2021) using z-scores vs your 30-day baseline: elevated RHR, suppressed HRV, and elevated respiration rate. A composite z-score above 2 may be a pre-symptomatic illness signal — before you feel sick.

RHR + HRV-inverted + respiration z-scores (Quer 2021)

Recovery Debt iTracks cumulative energy deficit when your morning Body Battery falls below your personal baseline. Persistent debt means your body isn't recovering fully between days.

Inflammation Index iA composite z-score combining elevated RHR, elevated respiration rate, and reduced sleep efficiency. Higher values suggest systemic stress, illness, or heavy recovery demands.

RHR + respiration − sleep efficiency, z-scored

Sleep Regularity Index iMeasures how consistent your sleep timing is day-to-day (0–100). High scores are linked to better metabolic and cardiovascular health — even independent of sleep duration (Windred 2024).

≥87 regular · <60 irregular

7-day rolling consistency of sleep midpoint (Windred 2024)

Social Jet Lag iDifference between your weekday and weekend sleep midpoints. A gap >1 hour is linked to metabolic dysfunction, mood issues, and cardiovascular risk.

Weekday vs weekend sleep midpoint

Stress Resilience Score iHow effectively your body recovers from daily stress. Higher scores mean your autonomic nervous system is bouncing back efficiently from stressors.

Daily, normalised against your 30-day stress baseline

Body Battery Decay Slope iHow fast your body battery depletes during waking hours, expressed as points per hour. Steeper decay = faster autonomic drain.

Points lost per hour — steeper = faster autonomic drain

Behavior Recovery Cost iMedian number of days for your HRV to return within 0.5σ of baseline after each logged behavior. Longer bars = harder impact on recovery.

Median days for HRV to return within 0.5σ of baseline
